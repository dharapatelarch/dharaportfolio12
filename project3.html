The Loom Yard

Concept : To create a self-sustaining artisan micro-habitat that celebrates the weaving heritage of Bhuj, while also supporting local artisans with workspaces, living quarters, and a cultural platform to showcase their crafts. This design is both functional and cultural, reflecting traditional values and contemporary community needs.

A self-sustaining artisan micro-habitat that celebrates the weaving heritage of Bhuj, integrating living, working, and exhibition spaces to support local artisans while preserving tradition and fostering community interaction.

Total Site Area : 8015.45 M2 - Proposed Site Area : 900 M2

Situated on the edge of Bhimraonagar in Bhuj, this site lies in a region steeped in textile craftsmanship, home to generations of skilled artisans. The proximity to artisan settlements means this space is not just a workshop—it's a community landmark, embedded in the social and economic fabric of the neighborhood.
